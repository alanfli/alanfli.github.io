Projects

National Insitute of Standards and Technology, Summer 2016

Highly charged ions are important in physics research for accurately measuring fundamental constants. These experiments are carried out in traps that use extremely powerful electric fields to keep the ions in place. Our team investigated the theoretical possibility of an electric field ionization in either a Penning Trap or a Radio Frequency Paul Trap. I collected and processed massive amounts of data, used formulas I dervied from academic research, and visualized the data with informatives graphics.

National Institutes of Heath, Summer 2015

Our team explored the possible novelty of protein MLKL in TNF-induced cell necroptosis, which is a special form of cell death that is currently under review as a possible treatment for tumors. The team concluded that MLKL was indeed a vital protein in TNF-induced necroptosis. The team then investigated the novelty of another protein, RAR-γ. I conducted biological experiments on rats and cell cultures, prepared samples, and examined data.
